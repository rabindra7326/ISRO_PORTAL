Module 3: GIS Analysis & Applications

Understanding spatial analysis and real-world GIS applications

Course Content

1. Spatial Analysis

Spatial analysis is the process of examining the locations, attributes, and relationships of features in spatial data. It helps in identifying patterns and trends on maps, which is useful for decision-making.

2. Overlay Analysis

Overlay analysis involves combining multiple data layers to produce new information. For example, combining soil and rainfall maps can help determine suitable areas for agriculture.

3. Buffering

Buffering creates zones around map features such as roads or rivers. It is used to analyze areas within a specific distance, such as identifying areas affected by pollution.

4. Network Analysis

Network analysis is used to study transportation routes and find the shortest or fastest paths. It is widely used in GPS navigation systems.

5. Applications of GIS

GIS is used in various fields such as urban planning, environmental monitoring, disaster management, agriculture, and transportation systems.

6. Real-world Uses

Governments and organizations use GIS for planning cities, managing natural resources, and responding to emergencies like floods and earthquakes.
