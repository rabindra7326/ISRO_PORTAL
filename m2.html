Module 2: Satellites and Sensors

Complete detailed course content

Detailed Course Content

1. Introduction to Satellites

Satellites are objects placed in orbit around the Earth to collect and transmit data. They play a key role in remote sensing by capturing images and information about the Earth's surface.

2. Types of Satellites

Satellites are mainly classified into geostationary and polar satellites based on their orbit and function.

3. Geostationary Satellites

These satellites remain fixed over a single location on Earth and are used for weather monitoring and communication.

4. Polar Satellites

These satellites move from pole to pole and provide complete coverage of the Earth over time.

5. Satellite Orbits

Satellite orbits define the path followed by satellites around the Earth.

6. Low Earth Orbit (LEO)

Satellites in LEO are closer to Earth and provide high-resolution images.

7. Medium Earth Orbit (MEO)

Used mainly for navigation systems like GPS.

8. High Earth Orbit (HEO)

Satellites at high altitude provide wide-area coverage.

9. Introduction to Sensors

Sensors are devices that capture data from the Earth's surface.

10. Types of Sensors

Sensors can be passive or active depending on how they collect energy.

11. Passive Sensors

They detect natural energy such as sunlight reflected from objects.

12. Active Sensors

They emit their own energy and measure the reflected signals.

13. Imaging Sensors

Used to capture images of the Earth's surface.

14. Non-Imaging Sensors

Used to measure specific parameters like temperature.

15. Data Collection Process

Satellites collect data and transmit it to ground stations.

16. Data Transmission

Collected data is sent to Earth using communication systems.

17. Data Processing

Raw data is processed into usable images and information.

18. Data Analysis

Processed data is analyzed for decision-making.

19. Applications

Satellite data is used in agriculture, weather, and mapping.

20. Importance

Satellites and sensors are essential for modern remote sensing.
